## Description

**A 4-minute gentle animated story about a child rescued by a mother bear and her return to the forest twenty winters later. Designed for calm viewing — no flashing lights, no loud sounds, no scary moments, no fast cuts. Soft narration, warm music, slow scene transitions. Safe for sensory-sensitive kids and great for bedtime.**

Two acts:
00:00 — Act One: a little girl is lost in a winter storm and rescued by a mother bear. She hibernates with the bear family through winter and is reunited with her parents in spring.
02:00 — Act Two: twenty winters later, she returns to the forest as a grown woman to find the bear family again.

The narration is calm and steady throughout. The animation uses soft watercolor (Act One) and warm photoreal cinematography (Act Two). No frightening creatures, no chase scenes, no startling sounds.
